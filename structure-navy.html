Rank Structure of Indian Army

The profession in Indian Armed Forces is a unique vocation of experts who are entrusted to defend the Constitution and the rights and interests of India. More than an indication of pay grade.Ranks provide a system of leadership that indicates a Soldier's level of expertise, responsibility and authority. As they say in Indian armed forces " You wear your heart on your sleeve and your resume on your uniform". Rank defines a soldier while Insignia is a distinguishing mark and a badge of authority or honor.

COMMISSIONED OFFICER

ADMIRAL OF THE FLEET

Insignia- Contain one thick and four thin gold braid stripes (loop on the topmost stripe).
In many nations the rank is reserved for wartime or ceremonial appointments. No officer of the Indian Navy has yet been conferred this rank. The title admiral of the fleet can trace its origins to the Middle Ages, where the title was typically granted to a nobleman who was appointed by a monarch to raise and command a navy for a specific campaign.

Retirement- There is no retirement age for this rank. It is awarded lifelong.

ADMIRAL

Insignia- Shoulder Board – The rank Insignia of Admiral is Four-star beneath crossed sword and baton, surmounted by Emblem of Indian.
Sleeves Insignia – Contain one thick and three thin gold braid stripes (loop on the topmost stripe)
The Admiral is known as The Chief of the Naval Staff (Admiral) & is the commander and the highest-ranking officer in the Indian Navy. Admiral in Indian Navy is a four-star rank. Admiral rank is equivalent to “General” in Indian Army & “Air Chief Marshal” in Indian Airforce.

Retirement- 62 years or three years of tenure, whichever is earlier

VICE ADMIRAL

Insignia- Shoulder Board – The rank Insignia of Rear Admiral is Three-star beneath crossed sword and baton, surmounted by Emblem of Indian.
Sleeves Insignia – Contain one thick and two thin gold braid stripes (loop on the topmost stripe).
Vice Admiral is a senior officer rank which is senior to a rear admiral and junior to an admiral. The Vice Chief of the Naval Staff is second in command of the Indian Navy and is a Three-star Officer. He is responsible for all the operations of naval related tasks. Vice Admiral rank is equivalent to “Lieutenant-general” in Indian Army & “Air Marshal” in Indian Airforce.

Retirement- 60 years

REAR ADMIRAL

Insignia- Shoulder Board – The rank Insignia of Rear Admiral is Two-star beneath crossed sword and baton, surmounted by Emblem of Indian.
Sleeves Insignia – Contain one thick and one thin gold braid stripe (loop on the topmost stripe).
A Rear Admiral in Indian navy is senior and two star rank naval officer, appointed in higher Naval commands.The rank insignia for a rear-admiral is two stars beneath crossed sword and baton, all surmounted by Emblem of India, worn on shoulder boards.

Retirement- 58 years

COMMODORE

Insignia- A Commodore insignia has one thick gold braid strip with a gold ring above it on a black background.
Traditionally, "commodore" is the title for any officer assigned to command more than one ship at a time, even temporarily, much as "captain" is the traditional title for the commanding officer of a single ship even if the officer's official title in the service is a lower rank. As an official rank, a commodore typically commands a flotilla or squadron of ships as part of a larger task force or naval fleet commanded by an admiral.

Retirement- 56 years

CAPTAIN

Insignia- Captain insignia contain four gold braid stripes (loop in the top stripe) on a black background.
Captain rank in Indian Navy Ranks is equivalent to “Colonel” in Indian Army and “Group Captain” in Indian Airforce. A navy officer who commands a ship is called by naval custom as “captain”. Captain rank is one rank senior to Commander and one rank junior to Commodore.

Retirement- 56 years

COMMANDER

Insignia- Commander insignia contains three medium gold braid stripes (loop on the top stripe).
Commander is a common naval and air force officer rank. Commander is also used as a rank or title in other formal organisations, including several police forces.

Retirement- 54 years

LIEUTENANT COMMANDER

Insignia- Lieutenant Commander insignia consists of two medium gold braid stripes (loop on top stripe) with one thin stripe in between on a black background.
This rank is equivalent to "Major" in Indian Army and "Squadron leader" in Indian Air force.

Retirement- 52 years

LIEUTENANT

Insignia- Lieutenant insignia has two medium gold braids stripes (top stripe with loop) on a black background.
This rank is equivalent to "Captain" in Indian Army and "Flight Lieutenant" in Indian Air force.

Retirement- 52 years

SUB-LIEUTENANT

Insignia- Sub-lieutenant insignia consists of one medium gold braid stripe with executive curl.
It is rank earned after getting commissioned into the force from the academy. It is equivalent to Lieutenant in Indian Army and Flying officer in Indian Air Force.

Retirement- 52 years

JUNIOR COMMISSIONED OFFICER

MASTER CHIEF PETTY OFFICER- 1st CLASS

Insignia-

Master Chief Petty Officer First Class is the senior-most rank of junior commissioned officer in the Indian Navy. Although they are commissioned officers but play a role similar to that of the most senior non-commissioned officers.
Master Chief Petty Officer (1st class) rank is equivalent to “Subedar Major” in Indian Army & “Master Warrant Officer” in Indian Airforce..

Retirement- 57 years

MASTER CHIEF PETTY OFFICER- 2nd CLASS

Insignia-

A Master Chief Petty Officer Second Class is an officer in the Indian Navy who is designated as a junior commissioned officer (JCO) distinguished from the commissioned ones and is a non-commissioned senior officer. Master Chief Petty Officer (2nd class) rank is equivalent to “Subedar” in Indian Army & “Warrant Officer” in Indian Airforce. An officer of Indian navy gets this rank after the promotion of chief petty officer rank.

Retirement- 57 years

CHIEF PETTY OFFICER

Insignia-

A chief petty officer is a junior commissioned officer (JCO) rank in the Indian Navy. They are in between the commissioned and non-commissioned ranks. Responsibility of a Chief Petty Officer is to train junior officers and leading his division of sailors and petty officers.
Chief Petty Officer rank is equivalent to “Naib Subedar” in Indian Army & “Junior Warrant Officer” in Indian Airforce.

Retirement- 52 years

NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER

PETTY OFFICER

Insignia-

A Petty Officer in Indian Navy is a non- commissioned officer. A Petty Officer rank is equal to the Sub Inspector of police in the Indian Police service. A Petty officer is senior to Leading Rate and Subordinate to Chief Petty Officer.
Petty Officer rank is equivalent to “Havildar” in Indian Army & “Sergeant” in Indian Airforce. A Petty officer is responsible for the training and recruitment of new members in Indian Navy.

Retirement- 52 years

LEADING SEAMAN

Insignia-

Leading Seaman is a junior non-commissioned rank or rate in indian navy and other Commonwealth countries. When it is used by NATO nations, Leading Seaman has the rank code of OR-4.

Retirement- 52 years

SEAMAN

An ordinary seaman (OS) is a naval rating of the deck department of a ship. The position is an apprenticeship to become an able seaman, and has been for centuries.This rank is equivalent to sepoy in Indian Army and Airmen in Indian Air force.

Retirement- 52 years
