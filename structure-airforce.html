Rank Structure of Indian Airforce

The profession in Indian Armed Forces is a unique vocation of experts who are entrusted to defend the Constitution and the rights and interests of India. More than an indication of pay grade.Ranks provide a system of leadership that indicates a Soldier's level of expertise, responsibility and authority. As they say in Indian armed forces " You wear your heart on your sleeve and your resume on your uniform". Rank defines a soldier while Insignia is a distinguishing mark and a badge of authority or honor.

COMMISSIONED OFFICER

MARSHAL OF THE IAF

Insignia-

Marshal of the air force is the English term for the most senior rank in a number of air forces. The ranks described by this term can properly be considered marshal ranks. Marshal of the Indian Air Force Arjan Singh, DFC, Padma Vibhushan awardee (born 15 Apr 1919 as Arjan Singh Aulakh) is the only officer of the Indian Air Force to be promoted to five-star rank, equal to a Field Marshal, to which he was promoted in 2002. There is no retirement age for this rank.

Retirement- There is no retirement age for this rank. It is awarded lifelong.

AIR CHIEF MARSHAL

Insignia-

Also known as Chief of the Air staff , He is the professional head and the commander of the Indian Air Force.The position is abbreviated as CAS in the Indian Air Force cables and communication, and is usually held by a four-star air officer of the rank Air Chief Marshal. It is a four star rank in indian airfoce.

Retirement- 62 years or three years of tenure, whichever is earlier

AIR MARSHAL

Insignia-

Air Marshal (Air Mshl or AM) is a Three- star officer rank with NATO ranking code of OF-8. Air Marshal rank in Indian Airforce is equivalent to the rank of “Lieutenant General” in Indian Army and “Vice Admiral” in Indian Navy.
It is a rank Below Air Chief Marshal and Above Air Vice Marshal. The officer at the rank of Air Marshal holds very senior appointments such as commander- in – Chief of Indian Airforce formation.

Retirement- 60 years

AIR VICE MARSHAL

Insignia-

Air vice-marshal (AVM) is a two-star air officer rank which originated in and continues to be used by the Royal Air Force.The rank is also used by the air forces of many countries which have historical British influence and it is sometimes used as the English translation of an equivalent rank in countries which have a non-English air force-specific rank structure.

Retirement- 58 years

AIR COMMODORE

Insignia-

Air commodore (abbreviated as Air Cdre in the IAF) is a one-star rank and the most junior general rank of the air-officer which originated in and continues to be used by the Royal Air Force.

Retirement- 56 years for Flying Branch and 57 years for other branches

GROUP CAPTAIN

Insignia-

It is usually abbreviated Gp Capt. While the rank originated in the British Royal Air Force (RAF),group captain is a rank used by the air forces of many Commonwealth and other countries that have been influenced by British military culture. It is sometimes used as the English translation of an equivalent rank in countries which have a non-English air force-specific rank structure.

Retirement- 54 years for Flying Branch and 57 years for other branches

WING COMMANDER

Insignia-

Wing Commander (Wg Cdr) is a senior commissioned rank with NATO ranking code of OF-4. Wing Commander ranks immediately above Squadron Leader and below Group Captain.
This rank is equivalent to the rank of “Commander” in Indian Navy and “Lieutenant Colonel” in Indian Army. Wing Commander commands a wing that administrative subdivision.

Retirement- 52 years for Flying Branch; 54 years for Ground Duty Branches apart from meteorological and education branches; and 57 years for Meteorological and Education branches

SQUADRON LEADER

Insignia-

Squadron Leader (Sqn Ldr) is a commissioned rank in Indian Airforce and is the most junior of the senior officer rank with NATO ranking code of OF-3.
Squadron Leader ranks immediately above Flight Lieutenant and below Wing Commander. This rank is equivalent to the rank of “Lieutenant Commander” in Indian Navy and “Major” in Indian Army.

Retirement- NA

FLIGHT LIEUTENANT

Insignia-

Flight Lieutenant (Flt Lt) is a junior commissioned rank with NATO ranking code of OF- 2. A Flight Lieutenant ranks above Flying Officer and below Squadron Leader in Indian Airforce.
This rank is equivalent to the rank of “Lieutenant” in Indian Navy and “Captain” in Indian Army.

Retirement- 57 years

FLYING OFFICER

Insignia-

Flying Officer (Fg Off) is a Junior Commissioned rank with NATO ranking code of OF- 1. Flying officer rank in Indian Air Force Ranks is equivalent to the rank of “Sub- Lieutenant” in Indian Navy and “Lieutenant” in Indian Army.

Retirement- 54 years

JUNIOR COMMISSIONED OFFICER

MASTER WARRANT OFFICER

Insignia-

This is the rank in which an officer is designated by warrant, unlike commissioned officers who get designated as an officer by a commission. It is the highest rank in Junior commissioned officers.

Retirement- NA

WARRANT OFFICER

Insignia-

A warrant officer is a Senior Non Commissioned Officer(SNCO) and a senior Air warrior(airman). Warrant officer is part of warranted ranks which consists of Junior warrant officer who gets promoted to Warrant officer and then to Master warrant officer. A warrant officer's role depends on his trade.

Retirement- NA

JUNIOR WARRANT OFFICER

Insignia-

Junior warrant officer rank is below the warrant officer and this rank is equivalent to Naib subedar in Indian Army.

Retirement- NA

NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER

SERGEANT

Insignia-

Sergeant (Sgt) is a non-Commissioned officer placed above the rank of corporal and below the rank of Junior warrant officer in Indian Airforce. Sergeant rank is equivalent to the rank of “Petty officer” in Indian Navy and “Havaldar” in Indian Army.

Retirement- NA

CORPORAL

Insignia-

Corporal rank is a Non- commissioned Indian Air Force Ranks and comes under NATO ranking code of OR-4. It is a rank given to an airman who is above Leading Aircraftsman and below sergeant. Corporal rank is equivalent to the rank of “Able Seaman” in Indian Navy and “Naik” in Indian Army.

Retirement- NA

LEADING AIRCRAFTSMAN

Insignia-

Leading Aircraftsman (LAC) is junior- commissioned rank in Indian Airforce with NATO ranking Code of OR-2. It comes between an Aircraftsman and a Corporal. Leading Aircraftsman rank is equivalent to the rank of “Leading Seaman” in Indian Navy and “Lance Naik” in Indian Army.

Retirement- NA

AIRCRAFTSMAN

Insignia-

Aircraftsman (AC) is the lowest rank in Indian Airforce with NATO ranking code of OR-1. It is just a training rank only. Aircraftmen do not wear any rank Insignia. This rank is equivalent to the rank of “Seaman” in Indian Navy and “Sepoy” in Indian Army.

Retirement- NA
